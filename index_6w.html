Deva

God realm: self-absorbtion and pride

Deva realm is characterized by fixation and meditative absorption, physical and mental bliss. You experience breakthroughs, tremendous achievements and pleasure. Hope and fear no longer apply to Deva state. You experience blissful union (with God, Universe, Nature etc.). Everything is beautiful and loving. All is in harmony. However, this union is achieved not with God or Universe but with one's own ego image. This is an achievement of complete identity with one's own ego, elimination of all internal conflicts by ful identification.

There is no pain and suffering in this state. Fear and anxiety only start to creep in after disilussionment, after the realization of impermanent nature of the state. The state that was experienced as final and absolute. In the end, suffering settles in as the fear of loss and the concern for maintenance of this state.

The world is completely ignored in Deva state. Essentially, you are in a company with yourself. In fact, the tiniest glimpse of the outside world becomes your worst nightmare. There is an impenetrable wall of ignorance built around oneself and the world is ignored.

Asura

Demi-God realm: paranoia, jealousy, speed, competitiveness.

The dominant characteristic of this state is paranoia combined with extreme efficiency, energy and accuracy. It is associated with primal element of wind in Buddhist cosmology. Asuras are extremely competitive. They constantly try to achieve something and avoid all possible pitfalls without much preparation which requires highly developed sense of spontaneous creativity and freedom.

Everything is regarded as a game that needs to be won in the Asura realm and everyone is an opponent or an enemy. Extreme achievement mentality, speed and agressive strife to overpower and outsmart are the main driving forces. There is a constant need to reach and maintain complete control, covering all areas. A form of warlord mentality takes over the entire life force.

Relationships in the world are marked with constant jealousy, envy, intrigue and competition combined with an unbounded ambition. As a result Asuras suffer from self-condemnation for not "being the best", for not fulfilling the injunction. There is a constant outside threat and competition that needs to be outperformed, outmaneuvred, destroyed, exterminated. But this is a truly Sysiphean task for the number of the competition is legion. The more you get rid of them the more they pop up everywhere. This creates an atmosphere of hunt, chase and thrill saturated by aversion and aggression where it is you against the world.

Manusya

Human realm: selective passion, feeling of inadequacy, desire / sadness.

The central characteristic of Manusya realm is passion accompanied by the sense of poverty and the painful awareness of separateness between oneself and the object of one's longing. Constant yearning for the object of desire is accompanied by the sense of inadequacy and weakness. But this longing of Manusya realm is not only to have (something) but also very much to be(-come someone). You have an acute sense of lack coupled with self-consciousness. And the more refined your idea of the object of desire the more unbridgeable the sense of lack.

Compared to Deva and Asura realms, Manusya are more intellectually and conceptually minded. There is a high thought traffic and constant rumination taking place in the back of one's mind, critical examination and investigation. Manusya are very selective and discriminative, striving for intellectual understanding. But failure to grasp object intellectually adds to the suffering from dissatisfaction.

In Manusya realm, you are trying to get hold of the object of your desire. The distance between you and the object is the foremost source of your suffering. But this world is such that the object can never be reached. For one, it is but an image of your imagination, (not to be confused with hallucination), it is a concept. Even if you take hold of an object, the identification with the idea-concept fails. It turns out not to be the one you were striving for. Or, what you were really admiring has meanwhile shifted, molded into something else, changed its shape as well as location. The general dynamic here is that of an impossible and tragic love story between you and the world.

Tiryagyoni

Animal realm: close-mindedness, ignorance

Tiryagyoni is marked by blindness to one's own style (or the way one behaves, looks, sounds), to the way others see us. As Tiryagyoni you do not question yourself. Tiryagyoni are stubborn, predetermined and very serious. You would rather follow the established patterns and habits and are absolutely unwilling or even unable to look outside the given game. Tiryagyoni have uncritical approach towards their goals and what they do. Lying and finding excuses for your way of life is done with such unquestionable sincerity that it can be easily taken for honesty. As a matter of fact you even take pride in your ignorance and confusion, you regard it as straightforwardness. This gives Tiryagyoni great strength that helps them overpower others, draw them into their world.

Your ignorance as Tiryagyoni consists in having one particular aim and territory and being disinterested in the rest of the world. Tiryagyoni is very conservative, keeps to the familiar and is always suspicious of and condemning of the unfamiliar. The primary source of your pain is precisely the collapse of the familiar. If the force of the new is too strong and cannot be overpowered, Tiryiagyoni switches to ignorance, boycotting or playing dead.

Evenyour own self, is ignored, blocked. You are unceremoniously and directly dealing with the world like a tank. Tiryiagyoni has no self-consciousness. You cannot even recognize yourself in a mirror if you were confronted by your self-image. In fact a mirror or a glimpse of recognition of your own face is one of your worst nightmares. Tiryagiony builds an almost impenetrable wall of ignorance around their territory and then proceeds by completely ignoring oneself.

Preta

Hungry ghost realm: hunger, possessiveness, poverty mentality.

Preta realm is marked by the sense of lack or more like of an abysmal hole. You are obsessively concerned with expansion, acquisition, accumulation, consumption. Preta has a tiny mouth and a huge belly. They are always hungry for more, since it is impossible for them to fill their belly. Actually, it is the insatiable desire itself that induces the lack. It not only serves for perpetual crave but it also at the same time forms the abyss of lack.

Your suffering comes from the constant sense of hunger and lack as well as dissatisfaction from that which has been consumed. What you consume or acquire neither satisfies your craving nor reduces your lack. Actually, you may even feel disgusted by it afterwards. Preta is a battleground between the conflicting emotions of attraction and repulsion. Desire and the frustration of the desire. Your are striving to satisfy your insatiable hunger but just as much to get rid of it. All your accumulations and takeovers are but a failed attempt to end this race. But your hunger can no more be satisfied than a black hole can be filled. Preta is slowly eaten by their hunger-desire from within. Their story is a story of a heavy addict where an overdose seems like the only viable solution.

< Craving fixed in self. >

Naraka

Hell realm: hatred, anger, claustrophobia

Naraka realm is permeated by claustrophobic self-perpetuating anger and aggression. "Each time the crocodile bites his own tail, he is nourished by it; the more he eats, the more he grows. There is no end to it." Naraka is suffering from constant pain that is echoed by their claustrophobic anger. Your striving is to get rid of this hatred inside you, but this hatred is devouring you from inside while also being your sustaining life force. There is no escape from it. It is an endless pit, black hole. It is a story of terribly failed anger management.
